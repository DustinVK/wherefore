## Summary
Settled the visual identity: the logo is the three-dot "because" operator (two dots over one)
drawn as circles in teal, paired with "wherefore" in Space Grotesk weight 600, lowercase.
Replaces the earlier terminal-style "> wherefore" wordmark (JetBrains Mono, teal caret,
blinking cursor). Palette carried over from the dashboard and personal site.

## Decisions / outcomes
- Mark: three-dot "because" operator, drawn as geometry (circles, not a font glyph), teal.
- Wordmark: "wherefore" in Space Grotesk 600, lowercase. No prompt caret.
- Palette: #14151a charcoal ground, #3a9b9d / #4ab5b7 teal, #258a84 teal on light backgrounds,
  #f0eadc ink.

## Why
The terminal caret was too visually busy next to the three-dot mark. The decisive constraint
was favicon size: the dot mark drawn as geometry survives to 16px, which a nine-letter
wordmark in a mono font never could. Moving to a clean grotesk makes the product read as a
settled tool rather than a hacker sketch. The "because" operator is semantically precise --
wherefore records the reasons behind decisions.

## Alternatives considered
- "> wherefore" in JetBrains Mono with teal prompt and blinking cursor: rejected for being too
  busy with the mark and for failing at favicon sizes.
